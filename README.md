# AI Survival Guide

A curated list of skills that are **unlikely to be replaced by AI in the next 5-10 years**. Based on web research, real examples, and reasoning from authoritative sources (Harvard Business School, McKinsey, Workday, World Economic Forum).

---

## Why This Matters

- **83% of leaders** agree AI makes human skills more important, not less (Workday, World Economic Forum)
- **~12% of workplace skills** remain entirely human (McKinsey)
- **40% of core job skills** will be disrupted in five years (WEF Future of Jobs 2025)
- AI excels at pattern recognition and tasks with clear rules; it struggles with limited data, face-to-face interaction, nuance, and embodied experience

---

## Skill Categories (Web-Researched + Real Examples)

### 1. Physical Embodiment & Manual Dexterity

**Why AI struggles:** Moravec's Paradox—tasks humans consider "simple" (moving hands skillfully, feeling textures, adjusting grip) are extremely hard for machines. Manual/artisanal jobs have only **~13% probability** of AI replacement (WEF Future of Jobs 2025).

| Skill | Real Reason |
|-------|-------------|
| **魔术 (Magic)** | Requires live performance, misdirection, audience psychology, and real-time physical sleight-of-hand. The essence is the in-person wonder and shared disbelief—not reproducible by video or AI. |
| **人本身的锻炼 (Physical training / Body work)** | Exercise, sports, yoga, martial arts build strength, flexibility, and proprioception. Robots cannot share your sweat, breath, or pain; coaching requires embodied presence. |
| **Crafts & skilled trades** | Electricians, chefs, leatherworkers, shoemakers—tasks like hand-cutting, stitching, and custom assembly depend on "intuition, sensitivity, and human experience" (Arsutoria School). |

---

### 2. Human Social Connection

**Why AI struggles:** AI cannot genuinely experience empathy, build trust, or form relationships. It performs pattern prediction, not meaning-making. Non-verbal cues (hesitation, tone, body language) matter as much as words.

| Skill | Real Reason |
|-------|-------------|
| **人类社交 (Human social interaction)** | Harvard Business School: "Establishing and maintaining trust," "navigating interpersonal conflict," "understanding complex or competing motivations" remain human-only. Active listening and reading what *isn't* said are irreplaceable. |
| **Leadership & relationship-building** | Korn Ferry: Leaders with high emotional intelligence show retention rates nearly **30 points higher**. AI can optimize workflows; it cannot lead people through change. |
| **Coaching & mentorship** | Employees grow through human engagement, shared experience, and trust. AI can inform; it cannot mentor. |

---

### 3. Intimacy & Embodied Connection

**Why AI struggles:** Physical intimacy is inherently embodied, relational, and rooted in touch, presence, and mutual vulnerability. No algorithm can substitute for shared human experience.

| Skill | Real Reason |
|-------|-------------|
| **Intimacy / 床上功夫 / make love** | Intimacy requires two (or more) bodies, consent, chemistry, and emotional attunement. It is not a task to automate—it is a lived, relational practice. AI can generate text or imagery; it cannot provide the physical and emotional reality of human connection. |

---

### 4. Judgment & Ethics

**Why AI struggles:** AI performs best with clear parameters and right/wrong answers. Ethical judgment and moral reasoning depend on values, context, and lived experience.

| Skill | Real Reason |
|-------|-------------|
| **Ethical decision-making** | Deloitte: AI technologies bring severe ethical risks (data privacy, provenance). Workday: Ethical decision-making ranks **#1** among skills leaders value in an AI-driven future. |
| **Judgment in ambiguity** | Healx (biotech) uses AI to predict drug effectiveness, but *humans* decide which treatments advance to clinical trials. Making sound decisions in ambiguity requires weighing trade-offs and applying context where data falls short. |

---

### 5. Creativity & Innovation

**Why AI struggles:** AI recombines patterns; it does not invent from imagination or challenge assumptions. True creativity involves intention, lived context, and originality.

| Skill | Real Reason |
|-------|-------------|
| **Creative thinking** | WEF: Creative thinking ranks in **top five** core skills employers see growing through 2030. People decide which problems are worth solving; AI accelerates brainstorming, not intent. |
| **Challenging assumptions** | Reframing problems, balancing divergent and convergent thinking, empathizing with stakeholders—these remain human. |

---

## What to Learn

- **Physical:** Magic, sports, crafts, dance, hands-on trades  
- **Social:** Active listening, conflict resolution, mentorship, trust-building  
- **Relational:** Intimacy, presence, emotional attunement  
- **Judgment:** Ethics, decision-making under uncertainty  
- **Creative:** Problem reframing, originality, challenging defaults
