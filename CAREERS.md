# AI-Resilient Careers & Occupations

While skills are the foundation, how do they translate into actual jobs? Based on data from GitHub repositories analyzing AI exposure (such as Andrej Karpathy's US Job Market Visualizer and various AI-Resilient Occupation datasets), jobs with the **lowest digital AI exposure** share common traits: they require physical presence, manual dexterity, real-time human interaction, or deep emotional intelligence.

Here are the top categories of careers that are highly resilient to AI disruption in the next 5-10 years:

## 1. Healthcare & Physical Therapy
AI can assist with diagnostics and paperwork, but it cannot physically manipulate a patient's body or provide bedside empathy.
- **Physical Therapists & Massage Therapists:** Requires deep understanding of human anatomy, real-time physical feedback, and manual manipulation.
- **Nurses & Aides:** High demand for physical presence, emotional support, and rapid physical response to patient needs.
- **Surgeons:** While robotic surgery exists, it is human-operated. The physical dexterity and life-or-death judgment required are not fully automatable.

## 2. Skilled Trades & Construction
Governed by *Moravec's Paradox*, navigating chaotic physical environments is incredibly difficult for robotics.
- **Electricians & Plumbers:** Every house is wired/plumbed differently. Diagnosing and physically fixing issues in cramped, unpredictable spaces is highly AI-resistant.
- **Roofers & Carpenters:** Requires balance, physical strength, and adapting to weather and unique structural challenges.

## 3. Live Entertainment & Arts
The value of these roles comes from the *human* element—the shared experience between performer and audience.
- **Magicians & Illusionists:** Relies on physical sleight-of-hand, misdirection, and reading the audience's psychology in real-time.
- **Live Musicians & Theater Actors:** People pay for the human connection, the potential for mistakes, and the raw emotion of a live performance.

## 4. Human Services & Counseling
AI can act as a sounding board, but it lacks true empathy and lived human experience.
- **Psychologists & Therapists:** Building a therapeutic alliance requires genuine human connection, reading micro-expressions, and navigating complex emotional trauma.
- **Social Workers:** Requires navigating complex human systems, visiting homes, and making nuanced ethical judgments about human welfare.

## 5. High-Stakes Strategy & Leadership
AI is a tool for optimization, but humans must decide *what* to optimize.
- **CEOs & Founders:** Requires setting a vision, taking on legal/financial risk, and convincing other humans to follow.
- **Judges & Arbitrators:** Requires applying the spirit of the law, ethical reasoning, and understanding human context, not just pattern matching.
